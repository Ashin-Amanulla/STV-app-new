Voting Explained

How we do it?

The Single Transferable Vote (STV) method is a preferential voting system used in multi-seat elections. It aims to provide proportional representation by allowing voters to rank candidates in order of preference. In each round, a candidate must reach a minimum quota of votes to be elected. If a candidate receives more votes than the quota, the excess votes are redistributed to the voters' next preference. If a candidate does not reach the quota, the candidate with the fewest votes is eliminated, and their votes are transferred to the voters' next preference. This process continues until all seats are filled. STV is used in several countries, including Ireland and Australia, and is often praised for promoting a more diverse and representative parliament.
